I have deep knowledge of the PC hardware market and the gaming industry — mostly acquired through PC Part Picker fantasy builds and binging YouTube videos. This note is where I track the patterns.

## Observations

- **GPU pricing is a sentiment index.** MSRP is fiction; street price tells you about supply chains, crypto cycles, and AI demand all at once. Watching it is basically free training for [[notes/financial-physics-simulations|market analysis]].
- **The best value is always one generation behind** — right up until it isn't, when the used market gets flooded and last-gen prices crater.
- **RAM is the cheapest flex per dollar.** The dream remains: 128GB, shown to friends, used at 12%.

## Why I care

Convincing someone to build a PC instead of buying prebuilt means I get to do the fun part — the assembly — while they pay for the parts. Everybody wins.
