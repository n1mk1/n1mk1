Games like *Civilization 7* and *Frostpunk* push me to manage multiple ideas and datasets simultaneously — and I think that's genuinely transferable.

## The skills underneath

- **Prioritization under uncertainty.** Every turn is a resource-allocation problem with incomplete information. You never have enough production, and you never have enough compute either.
- **Dashboard reading.** A 4X mid-game is a dozen interlocking metrics (economy, research, military, diplomacy). Learning to glance at them and know *which one is about to become a problem* is the same skill as reading a monitoring dashboard or a [[notes/financial-physics-simulations|simulation output]].
- **Long-horizon planning with short-horizon execution.** You commit to a victory condition 200 turns out, but you only ever act one turn at a time.

## Frostpunk specifically

Frostpunk adds the constraint that *every* metric is also a moral choice, which is a good reminder that optimization targets are decisions, not facts.
